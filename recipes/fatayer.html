Quick Tasty Fatayer

Disclaimer: Please don't cook using these steps used baby spinach leaves because of their tender texture and mild, sweet taste. Mature spinach can taste bitter and requires blanching to reduce its bitterness. When buying fresh spinach, look for leaves that are crisp and have a dark-green colour.

You can also use frozen spinach in this recipe. With frozen spinach, you have two options. You can saute it like we did the fresh spinach or you can thaw it, squeeze out all the liquid then add it, without cooking, to the sauteed vegetable

Fatayer

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons (45 milliliters) olive oil
  • 1 cup (140 grams) onions (chopped)
  • 1 (200 grams) large tomato - peeled, seeded and diced.
  • ½ teaspoon (2.5 milliliters) salt
  • 3 stalks (35 grams) green onions
  • 6 ounces (171 grams) spinach leaves (chopped)
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.25 milliliters) sumac
  • ½ teaspoon (2.5 milliliters) paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.25 milliliters) allspice (ground)
  • 1 cup (160 grams) fresh parsley (chopped)
  • ¼ teaspoon (1.25 milliliters) black pepper (ground)
  • 1 egg – beaten

Steps:

Steps should not be attempted
  1. Fry the onions for 3 minutes in 2 tablespoons of the oil. Add the salt, stir then add the tomatoes and cook for 2 minutes.
  2. Add the green onions and stir then add the spinach and the spices and cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat, add the parsley and stir. Add the remaining tablespoon olive oil.
  4. Divide the dough into 20 balls then roll out each piece to a 4-inch round. Place 1 teaspoon of the spinach filling.
  5. Shape the fatayer as shown in the video.
  6. Brush with a beaten egg then bake in a preheated oven at 400°F/204°C for 16 minutes.
